My view regarding the delivery of valuable software

The goal

Some time ago, a new work mate arrived to the office. He taught me a new concept called "DevOps". I had never heard about it before. At the beginning I though it was just another tool or methodology but nothing else. I soon realized DevOps has a great potential when I realized that it aims to close a development virtuous cycle and to get more frequently feedbacks. It fosters various team to work together. As a Developer/Operational guy I definitively have to consider it!!

A new world of new terms, concepts was unveiling in front of my eyes.

I want to expose the main ideas and some tools that I have gathered along my learning process.

I like to think about all of these concepts starting from a key principle in the agile movement:
Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.
Agile Manifesto

Despite it may seem to be all about software development it goes beyond. Where the software runs?(Ops team) Who knows the business case/user valuable think that the software has to cover?(business team)...

This broad principle encompasses development, operational and business teams among other teams as well.

The feedback loop

To satisfy our customers we have to provide:

early and continuous delivery of valuable software

We have defined a pipeline to make early and continuous delivery possible (we will see tools later) but what about valuable software?

Now is where I think that business team comes into the scene! How do we measure valuable software? It will depend on the business model. If the product that the customer requests is a mobile application the downloads count could be the right metric for the feedback loop:

  • more downloads --> more valuable software
  • more users --> more valuable software
  • more subscriptions --> more valuable software
  • more renewals --> more valuable software
  • .......

And the feedback loop could be a combination of metrics.

Eric Viles wrote on his book Lean startup about these metric and he alerts you about the consequences of taking bad metrics: vanity metrics & action metrics. Take care about your feedback loop or you will be building a vicious cycle that leads to the agile bankruptcy!

Dedicated to: the new work mate that arrived to the office and allowed me to start this travel.
